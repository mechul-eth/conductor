# Market Intelligence

## Market Overview

Mid-market SaaS decision-logging is an emerging category. Before Notion and Slack pushed the bar on "where does work happen," decision records lived in wikis nobody read. The category is now splitting into: (a) general-purpose doc tools that do decisions incidentally (Notion, Coda); (b) engineering-specific tools (Linear's docs, GitHub Discussions); (c) purpose-built decision logs (us, Decisive, a handful of bootstrapped competitors). ## Competitors

### Decisive
- **What they do:** Purpose-built decision log with tight Notion integration.
- **Strengths:** Best-in-class Notion import/export, enterprise-feeling UI. [external]
- **Weaknesses:** Slack integration is one-way (we post to Slack; they import from Slack). Users complain about latency. [external, user-shared-link]
- **Pricing:** $18/seat/mo flat; no free tier. [external]

### Linear Docs
- **What they do:** Docs inside Linear — decisions live next to issues.
- **Strengths:** Zero-friction for Linear-native engineering teams. [user-stated]
- **Weaknesses:** Locked to Linear; no multi-team use case (support/sales don't live in Linear). [user-implied]
- **Pricing:** Bundled with Linear seats. [external]

### Notion
- **What they do:** Not a decision tool, but teams use it as one.
- **Strengths:** Already adopted; zero new-tool tax. [user-stated]
- **Weaknesses:** No structured decision semantics, no notification routing, no audit trail. [user-stated]
- **Pricing:** $8/seat/mo standard. [external]
